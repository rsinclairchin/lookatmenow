Walk-A-Tour

A mobile web app that allows users to take museum-style audio tours of San Francisco!
Users can upload audio snippets offering interesting tidbits or stories about a place in San Francisco. These snippets can then be strung together into audio tours for other users to rate and enjoy!
This app was built at a daylong hackathon using Ruby, Sinatra, MySQL, and the Google Maps API.

TasteMaker

An iOS mobile app for people who want to explore different wines.
Users scan barcodes to find the wine they are currently drinking, and are then are prompted to rate different aspects of that wine. Using this data, TasteMaker develops a user taste profile and a user history. From here, TasteMaker is able to recommend wines that fit a similar profile to the user.
Built as a final project for Dev Bootcamp over the span of a week, this app was my first look at Meteor, mobile development and MongoDB.

Food On The Move

A web app that shows all the food truck locations in San Francisco.
Food On The Move makes use of the Google Maps API to display data retrieved from SF OpenData regarding the particular locations of food trucks. Users can select a food truck to see its rating on Yelp, as well as reviews.
My first hackathon project, completed at a daylong hackathon hosted by Dev Bootcamp.

Skip To My Loo

A web app that allows users to rate and review public restrooms.
Restrooms are displayed on a map, and users can log in to add restrooms, reviews or ratings. A sidebar displays information about restrooms, including features such as changing tables and purse hooks.
This full CRUD app was made in just a couple of days, and makes use of both Mapbox and OAuth.
